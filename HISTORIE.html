  1. 1459 Bucharest, the capital city of Romania, was officially founded in 1459 by Vlad III, also known as Vlad the Impaler or Vlad Dracula. The city's establishment was a strategic move, situated on the banks of the Dambovita River and positioned to serve as a fortress and trading hub. Over the centuries, Bucharest grew in importance, becoming the political, economic, and cultural center of Romania.
  2. 1595In 1595, Bucharest experienced a devastating fire that led to significant destruction within the city. This event resulted in the burning of many buildings and structures, and it was a challenging period for the city's residents. Fires in historical cities were particularly destructive due to the lack of advanced firefighting techniques. Bucharest, like many other cities of its time, had to rebuild and recover from the aftermath of this destructive fire.
  3. 1698In 1698, Bucharest became the capital of Wallachia, one of the three principalities that later formed modern-day Romania. This change in status marked a significant moment in Bucharest's history, as it became the political and administrative center of the region. Bucharest's role as a capital city would continue to evolve, ultimately leading to its status as the capital of the unified Romania in the 19th century.
  4. 1806-1812Bucharest, like many European cities, has experienced conflicts involving Russia at various points in its history. During the Russo-Turkish War of 1806-1812, Bucharest was occupied by Russian forces. This occupation played a role in shaping the city's development and its relationship with the major powers of the time.
  5. 1812 In 1812, Bucharest faced a devastating outbreak of plague that claimed the lives of approximately one-quarter of its population. Plague outbreaks were notorious for their high mortality rates and the profound impact they had on affected communities. The plague in 1812 would have had significant social and demographic consequences for Bucharest, with long-term effects on the city's population and society.

  7. 1916 During World War I, Bucharest, as the capital of Romania, faced occupation by German forces in 1916. This occupation had significant consequences for the city and the nation, including changes in administration and governance. It was a turbulent period in Bucharest's history, as it navigated the challenges of war.
